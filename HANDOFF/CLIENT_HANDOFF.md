# Kinship Landing Website — Owner's & Agency Handoff Guide

*A plain-English guide to how your website works, what you can change yourself, and what to hand a marketing agency. Prepared by Voxe Marketing.*

---

## How your website is built (the simple version)

Your website has two parts:

1. **The website itself** — the pages people see at kinshiplanding.com. These are built by a developer.
2. **Sanity (your content system / "CMS")** — a friendly editing dashboard where you change words, photos, room info, your menu, and more — *without* touching code.

When you edit something in Sanity and it gets "published," the website **rebuilds and updates automatically.** (Changes take a few minutes to appear.)

---

## What YOU can change yourself (no developer needed)

You log into your **Sanity dashboard** and edit. Two dashboards:

- **Main content** → `kinship-landing.sanity.studio`
  Rooms, homepage, About, Events, Explore listings, Gallery, contact info, social links, policies, and more.
- **Homa menu** → `kinship-homa-menu.sanity.studio` *(new!)*
  Your full Café + Bar menu — categories, items, descriptions, prices, and add-ons. Edit a price, add a dish, remove a seasonal item, drag to reorder.

> **Tip:** After editing, always click **Publish**. Changes appear on the live site a few minutes later.

### Quick "edit the Homa menu" steps
1. Go to **kinship-homa-menu.sanity.studio** and sign in.
2. Click **Homa Menu**.
3. Open a category (e.g., *Breakfast*), edit an item's name/price/description, or **+ Add item**.
4. Click **Publish**. Done — it'll show on the site shortly.

---

## What needs a DEVELOPER (not self-service)

These require a developer because they involve building, not just editing:

- **Brand-new page *types*** — e.g., a blog, or wedding/event landing pages. A developer builds the template *once*; after that, you can add posts/pages yourself in Sanity.
- **New editable fields** or restructuring how a page works.
- **Design/layout changes.**

So: *editing existing things = you. Building new kinds of things = a developer.*

---

## Handing this to a marketing agency

If an agency takes over creative/SEO/campaigns and you want them to have **full control**, give them these three things:

1. **Sanity access** — invite them as members of your Sanity project (so they can edit all content).
2. **The website code** — it lives in a GitHub repository (your developer/Voxe can transfer or grant access).
3. **The hosting + deploy access** — your **Hostinger** account, where the site is published.

With all three, an agency can edit content, build new pages (blog, weddings, events), and publish changes. Without the code + hosting, an agency can only edit existing content (still a lot!) but can't build new page types or change the design.

## Who to contact

- **Content/menu editing help, or building new pages:** your developer or Voxe Marketing.
- **Hosting/domain questions:** whoever manages your Hostinger account (currently Travis).
